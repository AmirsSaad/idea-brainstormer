Drone Video Relay Add-On with Beam-Tracking Antenna

drone-relay-beam-tracking 2026-05-23

Overview

A lightweight add-on module for a relay drone that receives FPV video at 5.8 GHz and retransmits it at 1.3 GHz to a ground station. The relay drone hovers 100–300 m above the FPV drone; FPV range up to 1 km. The key capability is RSSI-based beam tracking: a PTZ-mounted patch antenna actively steers toward the FPV drone below, without GPS and without any modification to the FPV drone.
  • Antenna diversity: lollypop omni (always on) + steered patch (primary when locked)
  • No GPS: hard design constraint — tracking is self-contained via RSSI gradient
  • Target weight: ~141 g total add-on (PTZ sub-assembly ~46 g, ~$33)

Tracking Algorithm

Phase 1 — Acquisition (scan)

  1. Lollypop omni active; establish baseline RSSI.
  2. Pan servo sweeps 270° in 10° steps (~28 positions). Hold 30 ms/step for VRX AGC to settle.
  3. Find peak RSSI azimuth → fine sweep ±20° in 2° steps.
  4. Tilt sweep: start at –15°, probe ±10° in 2° steps.
  5. Lock onto peak; switch RX to patch. Total: ~3–5 s.

Phase 2 — Track (dither hill-climbing, 100 ms loop)

  1. Sample RSSI at current position; rolling 5-sample average to reduce noise.
  2. Pan: probe +3° and –3°; move toward higher RSSI; hold if neither improves.
  3. Tilt: probe +2° and –2°; same logic.
  4. Apply IMU compensation: servo_cmd = world_target − drone_yaw / pitch.

Phase 3 — Loss recovery

  1. RSSI below threshold for >500 ms → switch RX to lollypop (signal maintained).
  2. Trigger new acquisition scan from current position.
  3. On re-lock, switch RX to patch; resume tracking.

IMU Attitude Compensation

  • MPU-6050 reads relay drone yaw, pitch, roll at 100 Hz via I2C.
  • Pan: world_azimuth − drone_yaw → servo command.
  • Tilt: world_elevation − drone_pitch → servo command.
  • Keeps patch on target as relay drone drifts, rotates, or responds to wind.

Implementation Flow

1 Hardware assembly — PTZ bracket & servo mount S
3D-print bracket in PETG; mount DS215MG (pan) and MG90S (tilt); attach 5.8 GHz patch to tilt arm.
Unknowns: Vibration coupling from rotors; rubber damping mounts may be needed.
Limits: Pan capped at 270°; relay drone yaw >135° exceeds servo range.
2 ESP32 firmware — RSSI ADC + servo PWM M
Read RSSI from VRX analog pin via ESP32 ADC (12-bit). Drive pan/tilt via PWM. Validate RSSI linearity.
Unknowns: RSSI pin behaviour varies by VRX module — bench-test first.
Limits: ESP32 ADC non-linearity near rails; voltage divider to keep RSSI in 0.1–3.1 V.
3 IMU integration — MPU-6050 attitude correction M
Mount MPU-6050 rigidly to drone frame (not PTZ). Read yaw/pitch via I2C; subtract drone attitude from servo commands.
Unknowns: Gyro yaw drift ~0.5°/min without magnetometer; acceptable for typical flights.
Limits: Motor/ESC magnetic interference may corrupt magnetometer if added later.
4 Acquisition algorithm — coarse + fine scan M
270° coarse pan sweep, fine ±20° sweep, tilt sweep. Output: locked (azimuth, elevation) in world frame.
Unknowns: Multipath can create secondary peaks; use peak-width as confidence score.
Limits: Acquisition takes 3–5 s; video relies on lollypop fallback during scan.
5 Track loop — dither hill-climbing M
100 ms control loop: dither pan ±3°, tilt ±2°, move toward RSSI gradient. Rolling 5-sample average.
Unknowns: Optimal dither step size depends on patch beamwidth — tune in field.
Limits: If VRX AGC settles >50 ms, effective loop rate must be reduced.
6 Loss recovery + antenna switching S
RSSI threshold detection; switch RX to lollypop on loss via RF switch; re-trigger acquisition scan.
Limits: Binary switching only — video may drop 1–2 frames per switch.
7 Field tuning — thresholds, dither size, noise floor L
Fly at various ranges and angles. Tune RSSI threshold, dither step, scan speed, AGC settle delay. Log RSSI + servo positions.
Unknowns: Real-world multipath, prop wash, EMI from 1.3 GHz VTX to RSSI circuit.
Limits: RSSI-only tracking cannot distinguish multipath from true signal; environment-dependent.

Risks & Open Questions

  • RSSI noise: multipath creates false peaks; use peak-width confidence scoring during acquisition
  • Pan hard limit: 270° covers ±135° yaw; relay drone spin beyond this loses tracking
  • IMU yaw drift: ~0.5°/min without magnetometer; fine for typical flights
  • Near-vertical geometry: FPV directly below relay pushes tilt to –90°; lollypop fallback handles this
  • EMI from 1.3 GHz VTX: shielding and physical separation of RSSI circuit required
  • VRX AGC latency: if >50 ms, tracking lags — validate on bench before tuning
  • Vibration: rubber damping mounts recommended for IMU and patch bracket
