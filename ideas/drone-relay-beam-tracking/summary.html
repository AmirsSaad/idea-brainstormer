Drone Video Relay Add-On with Beam-Tracking Antenna

drone-relay-beam-tracking 2026-05-23 · monopulse revision

Overview

A lightweight add-on for a relay drone that receives FPV video at 5.8 GHz from a non-cooperative military FPV drone below it, and retransmits it at 1.3 GHz to a ground pilot. The relay hovers 100–300 m above the FPV; FPV range up to 1 km lateral. The FPV emits only its video signal — no GPS, no telemetry. All tracking is passive.
  • Tracking: amplitude-comparison monopulse on a 2×2 cluster of CP patches → instantaneous 2-axis angular error (no dither, no scan during track)
  • Acquisition: wide-beam "finder" patch sweeps a coarse spiral until the emitter appears, then hands off bearing to the cluster
  • Sensor fusion: optional bore-sighted EO camera (OpenMV) provides redundant AoA and detects multipath/jamming via RF/EO disagreement
  • Fallback: CP omni "lollypop" maintains the relay link during acquisition and loss recovery
  • Target weight: ~165 g full add-on (cluster + gimbal + electronics)

Tracking Algorithm

Monopulse error signal

Four patches A (top-left), B (top-right), C (bottom-left), D (bottom-right), each squinted ~15° outward from cluster boresight. Each feeds an AD8318 log-detector → ESP32-S3 ADC. In log domain:

  • Σ = A + B + C + D — total received power; link-quality indicator and Σ-channel RX feed for the 1.3 GHz TX
  • Δaz = (B + D) − (A + C) — azimuth error (already in dB because detectors are log-output)
  • Δel = (A + B) − (C + D) — elevation error

Δ/Σ is the normalized error: zero on boresight, sign = direction, magnitude = how far off. A 2-axis PID drives the gimbal to null both Δ channels. Loop closed in <10 ms; servo bandwidth (~5–10 Hz) is the slow link.

Phase 1 — Cold acquisition (finder)

  1. Cluster + finder on the gimbal; finder patch ~70° HPBW.
  2. Spiral the gimbal over the downward hemisphere in 6–8 coarse cells, ~250 ms dwell.
  3. When finder Σ exceeds noise floor + 6 dB → bearing locked, hand off to cluster.
  4. Cluster monopulse converges to null in 1–2 PID cycles. Total cold-start budget: <3 s.

Phase 2 — Track (monopulse closed-loop)

  1. Sample all 4 log-detectors at >1 kHz (ESP32-S3 ADC handles this easily).
  2. Compute Σ, Δaz, Δel per sample; light low-pass filter (~50 Hz) for servo bandwidth.
  3. PID drives Δaz/Σ → 0 and Δel/Σ → 0 via the gimbal.
  4. BNO055 quaternion compensates relay-drone attitude (world-frame pointing maintained).
  5. Σ feeds the 1.3 GHz TX chain throughout — no link interruption.

Phase 3 — Loss recovery

  1. If Σ < noise floor for >500 ms, or |Δ|/Σ saturates → declare track lost.
  2. Switch RX path from cluster Σ to omni lollypop via SPDT (video continues).
  3. Re-enter Phase 1 acquisition while the omni carries the link.
  4. On re-lock, switch back to cluster.

EO/RF sensor fusion (optional)

  • OpenMV H7 R2 bore-sighted with cluster; tracks dark dot against sky.
  • EO reports azimuth/elevation over UART to the main MCU.
  • Agreement (RF AoA ≈ EO AoA) → tighten PID, raise confidence.
  • Disagreement → likely multipath or jamming on RF; bias the fused estimate toward EO.
  • If RF lost, EO holds the bearing until RF returns; if EO lost (cloud, dusk), RF carries.

Implementation Flow

1 CP patch cluster — design & fabricate L
Design 4× truncated-corner CP patches at 5.8 GHz (8 dBi, ~45° HPBW), polarization matched to FPV TX. Mount on a single PCB or alumina substrate with 15° outward squint. Add 5th wider-beam finder patch on the same plate.
Depends on: Nothing
Unknowns: Mutual coupling between adjacent cluster elements (model in EM solver); polarization sense of the actual FPV target
Limits: Cluster size sets minimum gimbal payload; squint angle is fixed in copper
2 RF front-end — log-detectors, combiner, filters L
Per patch: bandpass filter (rejects 1.3 GHz TX harmonics) → AD8318 log-detector → buffered analog out. Σ port: 4-way Wilkinson combiner → 5.8 GHz LNA → bandpass → into 1.3 GHz VTX RX input. SPDT RF switch on the VTX input selects between cluster Σ and omni lollypop.
Depends on: Step 1
Unknowns: Final filter rejection needed at 1.3 GHz harmonics (measure on bench); LNA NF
Limits: Wilkinson combiner introduces ~6 dB split loss — LNA gain must cover it
3 Gimbal — bracket, servos, mounting M
3D-print CF-PETG bracket; 2× KST DS215MG (pan + tilt, same part); rigid mount of cluster + finder + EO camera on a single bore-sighted plate. Damping mounts between gimbal base and drone frame.
Depends on: Step 1
Unknowns: Vibration coupling from rotors at cluster gain → may need fluid-filled damper
Limits: Servo backlash (~0.5°) sets the angular noise floor of the track loop
4 MCU firmware — ADC sampling, monopulse arithmetic, PID M
ESP32-S3 reads 4 ADC channels at 1 kHz, computes Σ / Δaz / Δel each sample, low-pass filters at 50 Hz, runs 2-axis PID outputting PWM to the servos. Quaternion in via I²C from BNO055; world-frame transform applied before PID.
Depends on: Steps 2, 3
Unknowns: PID gains (tune on bench against an RF source on a stage); ADC linearity across the 60 dB range
Limits: ESP32-S3 ADC nonlinearity near rails — use the AD8318 buffered output in its linear-in-dB region
5 Acquisition algorithm — spiral, threshold, handoff M
Acquisition uses Σ on the finder. Spiral pattern (6–8 cells over downward hemisphere) with 250 ms dwell. Threshold = noise-floor + 6 dB. On detection, slew cluster to bearing; cluster Δ/Σ takes over.
Depends on: Step 4
Unknowns: Noise floor in flight (motor EMI on the analog rails); may need adaptive threshold
Limits: If FPV emits in bursts (some military video), dwell must exceed the gap
6 EO camera fusion — OpenMV CV + Kalman fuse L
OpenMV H7 R2 runs onboard CV (dark-dot-against-sky tracker). Reports az/el over UART to ESP32-S3 at 30 Hz. EKF fuses RF Δ/Σ AoA and EO AoA into one state estimate. Disagreement flag triggers diagnostics (multipath vs jam vs cloud).
Depends on: Steps 4, 5
Unknowns: EO failure modes — clouds, dusk, ground clutter (target descending below skyline)
Limits: EO is an aid, not a primary; must degrade gracefully when one source drops
7 Loss recovery + antenna switching S
SPDT RF switch between cluster Σ and lollypop omni. Σ-threshold or |Δ|/Σ-saturation triggers switch and re-enters acquisition. Video carries on the omni during the gap.
Depends on: Steps 4, 5
Limits: Switch transient may drop 1–2 video frames
8 Bench & field tuning — error-slope calibration, PID, vibration L
Calibrate the monopulse error slope on a rotation stage with a 5.8 GHz CW source. Measure noise floor in flight, including motor EMI. Tune PID for the actual cluster + gimbal inertia. Validate cold acquisition, track, loss recovery, and EO fusion at various ranges and angles.
Depends on: Steps 1–7
Unknowns: Real-world multipath geometry; EMI from TX into the analog rails; prop-wash on gimbal
Limits: Bench calibration may not capture full multipath; iterate from flight logs

Risks & Open Questions

  • FPV polarization sense unknown: wrong-sense CP costs 20–30 dB. Confirm or hedge with dual-sense patches.
  • Mutual coupling between cluster elements: degrades monopulse error slope; needs EM modeling and ground-plane shaping.
  • Multipath at low altitude: Δ/Σ ratio is robust to fades, but specular ground reflection can still bias the AoA estimate near nadir.
  • 1.3 GHz TX harmonics into 5.8 GHz RX: 4th and 5th harmonics fall near the RX band. Needs bandpass filtering and physical separation.
  • Motor EMI on analog rails: log-detector outputs feed an ADC at sub-mV resolution. Shielded harness and star-ground required.
  • Servo backlash and gimbal vibration: sets the angular noise floor of the track loop; damped mounts essential.
  • EO failure modes: cloud, dusk, target-against-clutter (FPV below skyline). Fusion must degrade gracefully.
  • FPV may emit pulsed/bursty video: some military stacks burst-transmit. Acquisition dwell must exceed the inter-burst gap.
  • Regulatory: 1.3 GHz operation is ham-allocated and increasingly restricted near GNSS bands; document compliance per AO.
