Drone Video Relay Add-On with Beam-Tracking Antenna

drone-relay-beam-tracking 2026-05-23

Overview

A lightweight add-on module for a relay drone that receives FPV video at 5.8 GHz and retransmits it at 1.3 GHz to a ground station. The key capability is RSSI-based beam tracking: a PTZ-mounted patch antenna actively steers toward the FPV drone below, maximising received signal strength without requiring GPS or any modification to the FPV drone.
  • Relay geometry: relay drone 100–300 m above FPV drone; FPV range up to 1 km
  • Antenna diversity: omnidirectional lollypop (always on) + steered patch (primary when locked)
  • No GPS: hard design constraint — tracking is self-contained via RSSI
  • Target weight: ~141 g total add-on (PTZ sub-assembly ~46 g)

Tracking Algorithm

Phase 1 — Acquisition (scan)

  1. Omni lollypop receives signal; establish baseline RSSI.
  2. Pan servo sweeps 270° in 10° steps (~28 positions). Record RSSI at each step. Wait ~30 ms per step for AGC to settle.
  3. Find peak RSSI azimuth → narrow sweep ±20° around it in 2° steps.
  4. Tilt: start at –15°, sweep ±10° in 2° steps to find peak elevation.
  5. Lock onto peak position. Total acquisition time: ~3–5 s.

Phase 2 — Track (dither hill-climbing)

  1. Every 100 ms: sample RSSI at current position (rolling 5-sample average to reduce noise).
  2. Pan dither: probe +3° and –3°. Move toward higher RSSI. If neither improves, hold.
  3. Tilt dither: probe +2° and –2°. Move toward higher RSSI.
  4. IMU compensation applied every iteration: servo_cmd = world_target − drone_yaw/pitch.

Phase 3 — Loss recovery

  1. If RSSI drops below threshold for >500 ms → switch RX input to lollypop (signal maintained).
  2. Trigger new acquisition scan from current position outward.
  3. On re-lock, switch RX back to patch and resume tracking.

IMU Attitude Compensation

  • MPU-6050 provides relay drone yaw, pitch, roll at 100 Hz.
  • Pan servo command = (locked azimuth in world frame) − (drone yaw).
  • Tilt servo command = (locked elevation in world frame) − (drone pitch).
  • Keeps patch pointed at FPV drone even as relay drone drifts or rotates.

Implementation Flow

1 Hardware assembly — PTZ bracket + servo mount S
3D-print bracket, mount KST DS215MG (pan) and MG90S (tilt), attach 5.8 GHz patch to tilt arm.
Depends on: Nothing
Unknowns: Vibration coupling from rotors into patch; may need damping mounts
Limits: Pan capped at 270°; relay drone yaw >135° exceeds range
2 ESP32 firmware — RSSI ADC + servo PWM M
Read RSSI from VRX analog pin via ESP32 ADC (12-bit). Drive pan/tilt via PWM. Validate signal linearity.
Depends on: Step 1
Unknowns: VRX RSSI pin behavior may vary by module; test with your specific RX
Limits: ESP32 ADC has non-linearity near rails; add voltage divider to keep RSSI in 0.1–3.1 V range
3 IMU integration — MPU-6050 attitude correction M
Mount MPU-6050 rigidly to relay drone frame (not PTZ). Read yaw/pitch via I2C. Apply complementary filter. Offset servo commands by drone attitude.
Depends on: Step 2
Unknowns: Yaw drift from gyro integration; may need magnetometer for long hover sessions
Limits: MPU-6050 yaw drifts ~0.5°/min without mag; acceptable for short flights
4 Acquisition algorithm — coarse + fine scan M
Implement 270° coarse pan sweep + fine ±20° sweep + tilt sweep. Output: locked (azimuth, elevation) in world frame.
Depends on: Steps 2, 3
Unknowns: Multipath may create secondary RSSI peaks; use peak-width as a confidence score
Limits: Acquisition takes 3–5 s; video quality degrades during scan (omni fallback active)
5 Track loop — dither hill-climbing M
100 ms control loop: dither pan ±3°, tilt ±2°. Move toward RSSI gradient. Rolling 5-sample average for noise.
Depends on: Step 4
Unknowns: Optimal dither step size depends on patch beamwidth; tune in field
Limits: Algorithm reacts to RSSI — if VRX AGC is slow (>50 ms), tracking lags
6 Loss recovery + antenna switching logic S
RSSI threshold detection. Switch RX input to lollypop on loss. Re-trigger scan. RF relay switch (e.g. SKY13414) to toggle between patch and lollypop.
Depends on: Step 5
Unknowns: Switching glitch — video may drop 1–2 frames on antenna switch
Limits: Binary switching only; no true diversity combining
7 Field tuning — thresholds, dither size, noise floor L
Fly FPV drone at various ranges and angles. Tune RSSI loss threshold, dither step size, scan speed, AGC settle delay. Log RSSI and servo positions for analysis.
Depends on: Steps 1–6
Unknowns: Real-world multipath, prop wash on relay drone, EMI from VTX to RSSI circuit
Limits: RSSI-only tracking cannot distinguish multipath from true signal; environment-dependent tuning

Risks & Open Questions

  • Analog RSSI noise: multipath can create false peaks; needs noise-aware scan and peak-width confidence scoring
  • Pan limit at 270°: relay drone yaw >135° from center exceeds servo range; IMU can detect and warn, but cannot compensate
  • IMU yaw drift: MPU-6050 gyro integration drifts ~0.5°/min; acceptable for typical flight durations but degrades on long hovers
  • Near-vertical geometry: if FPV drone is directly below relay (<50 m lateral), patch is at –90° tilt — pattern degrades; lollypop fallback essential
  • EMI from 1.3 GHz VTX: transmitter on same platform as 5.8 GHz RX and RSSI circuit; shielding and physical separation needed
  • VRX AGC latency: if receiver AGC takes >50 ms to settle, dither-based tracking will lag; validate with specific VRX module
  • Vibration: rotor vibration on relay drone may couple into patch and IMU; rubber damping mounts recommended
